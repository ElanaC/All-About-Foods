Chinese Cuisine

Chinese cuisine is closely correlated with its culture and has now spread worldwide. The Four Great Traditions of chinese cuisine include Chuan (west), Lu (north), Yue (south), and Huaiyang (east). The color, smell, and taste all categorize aspects of traditional descriptions for chinese food. Traditional chinese medicine was able to draw in the vast attention to gastronomy for chinese society. During the Han dynasty, food preservations were studied to make it convenient for military purposes. This would include drying meat and grains, as well as roasted flat breads known as shaobing. Chinese people largely believe good food is important to enjoy with family to bring harmonious and close relationships. All foods have their own meaning and to express them fully, the highlight of people's days is going out to buy fresh ingredients everyday. Food served to guests can also show respect to them. Chinese cuisines also includes beliefs like never serving 4 or 10 dishes because in chinese dialet, it is a homophone to the word 'death'. Having these amounts of dishes would likely serve to be bad luck. Chinese culture has taken great influence around the world and its foods would inevitably spread as well. Other countries have altered popular foods to accomodated their tastebud. A prominent example would be a successful fast food chain called 'Panda Express'. Popular symbolic meanings to dishes would be describing them as luck, longevity and prosperity. Last but not least, unforgettable chinese cuisines will bring a sense of happiness, which would also be represented by oranges and other citrus fruits.

Have You Tried Traditional Chinese Foods?

Roasted Peking Duck

This is a traditional dish originating in Beijing Imperial times. It is usually prepared in front of customers, serving mostly its signiture crispy skin.

    Main Ingredients
  • Pekin Duck
  • Chinese Five Spice Powder
  • Soy and Hoisin Sauce
 Spring Rolls

They can be seen in Chinese New Year meals to represent bars of gold, to represent prosperity. They are called spring rolls because Chinese New Year starts the spring season in the lunar calendar.

    Main Ingredients
  • Spring Roll Wrappers
  • Fresh Vegetables (ex. carrots)
  • Protein of Choice (ex. shrimp)
 Dumplings

Many believe this signature dish was created by Zhang Zhongjing, a medical practitioner during the Han dynasty. Some have the tradition of eating it for breakfast in Chinese New Year, with its story of it curing cold and hunger.

    Main Ingredients
  • Dumpling Wrappers
  • Minced Meat
  • Vegetable Stuffing
 Dimsum (Brunch)
